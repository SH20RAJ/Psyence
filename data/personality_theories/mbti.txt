# Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

## Definition
A personality assessment based on Carl Jung's psychological types, categorizing individuals into 16 personality types using four dichotomous dimensions.

## Key Concepts
- Extraversion (E) vs. Introversion (I): energy source and focus
- Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N): information gathering preference
- Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F): decision-making approach
- Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P): lifestyle and structure preference

## Examples
- INTJ: strategic planners, independent, future-focused
- ESFP: spontaneous, people-oriented, present-focused
- ENTJ: natural leaders, goal-oriented, decisive
- ISFJ: supportive, detail-oriented, traditional

## Applications
- Team building and communication styles
- Career counseling and job fit
- Leadership development programs
- Relationship counseling and compatibility
- Educational learning style adaptation

## Related Theories / Biases
- Confirmation bias (fitting behavior to type)
- Barnum effect (accepting vague descriptions)
- Fundamental attribution error (type vs. situation)
- Self-fulfilling prophecy effects
