# Evolutionary Psychology

## Definition
The study of psychological traits and behaviors as adaptations that evolved to solve recurrent problems in human ancestral environments.

## Key Concepts
- Adaptive functions of psychological mechanisms
- Environment of Evolutionary Adaptedness (EEA)
- Sexual selection and mate choice
- Parental investment theory
- Cognitive modules and domain-specific adaptations

## Examples
- Fear of snakes and spiders (ancient threats)
- Preference for symmetrical faces (health indicators)
- Jealousy as mate-guarding behavior
- Altruism toward relatives (kin selection)

## Applications
- Understanding modern relationship dynamics
- Consumer behavior and marketing psychology
- Workplace hierarchy and leadership
- Risk assessment and decision making
- Social cooperation and conflict resolution

## Related Theories / Biases
- Loss aversion and risk perception
- In-group favoritism and out-group bias
- Status quo bias and change resistance
- Availability heuristic for threat assessment
