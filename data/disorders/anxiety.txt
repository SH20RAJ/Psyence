# Anxiety Disorders

## Definition
A group of mental health conditions characterized by excessive fear, worry, and related behavioral disturbances that significantly impair daily functioning.

## Key Concepts
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (persistent, excessive worry)
- Panic Disorder (recurrent panic attacks)
- Social Anxiety Disorder (fear of social situations)
- Specific Phobias (irrational fears of specific objects/situations)
- Fight-flight-freeze response system dysfunction

## Examples
- Avoiding social gatherings due to fear of judgment
- Physical symptoms: rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling
- Catastrophic thinking and worst-case scenario focus
- Avoidance behaviors that reinforce anxiety
- Sleep disturbances and concentration difficulties

## Applications
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) interventions
- Exposure therapy and systematic desensitization
- Mindfulness and relaxation techniques
- Medication management (SSRIs, benzodiazepines)
- Lifestyle modifications and stress management

## Related Theories / Biases
- Catastrophic thinking and probability overestimation
- Confirmation bias toward threat-related information
- Availability heuristic for negative outcomes
- Safety behaviors maintaining anxiety cycles
