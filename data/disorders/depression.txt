# Depression

## Definition
A mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities, significantly impairing daily functioning and quality of life.

## Key Concepts
- Major Depressive Disorder (clinical depression)
- Persistent Depressive Disorder (dysthymia)
- Cognitive triad: negative thoughts about self, world, future
- Neurochemical imbalances (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine)
- Behavioral activation and activity scheduling

## Examples
- Loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities
- Persistent sad mood and feelings of emptiness
- Sleep disturbances (insomnia or hypersomnia)
- Fatigue and decreased energy levels
- Difficulty concentrating and making decisions

## Applications
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for depression
- Interpersonal Therapy (IPT) approaches
- Antidepressant medication management
- Behavioral activation and pleasant activity scheduling
- Mindfulness-based interventions

## Related Theories / Biases
- Negative cognitive biases and distorted thinking
- Rumination and repetitive negative thinking
- Learned helplessness and attribution styles
- Confirmation bias toward negative information

## References
- Aaron Beck's cognitive theory of depression
- Martin Seligman's learned helplessness model
- DSM-5 diagnostic criteria and specifiers
- "Feeling Good" by David Burns (CBT techniques)
